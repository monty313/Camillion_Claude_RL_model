## Assumptions you should verify
1. **The PPO/MLP is Stable-Baselines3 (`stable_baselines3` 2.9.0), NOT a custom implementation.**
   So there is **no repo-level GAE / clipped-objective / value-loss function to unit-test** — that
   math lives inside SB3 (a widely-used, battle-tested library). The harness therefore verifies the
   **live PPO instance** (entropy, MLP shapes, gradient flow, determinism, a real 100-step train)
   instead of hand-checking GAE, and marks the pure-math items as **delegated-to-SB3** (not fake-passed).
2. **The observation is 479 float32 (contract v1.5.0), NOT 367.** The "367" in this audit's spec and in
   `CLAUDE.md` is stale (it was 367 at contract v1.1.0). The harness tests the REAL 479 and flags the
   doc drift as a fixable warning.
3. **Timeframes are 5 (`1m,5m,30m,4h,1d`), not 4.** The audit spec says 4; the harness uses the real 5.
4. **Core FTMO risk IS enforced** (daily 5%, total 10% static floor, 4% trailing) via `breach_detector`,
   and the env terminates on breach. The internal targets (2.5%/4%) sit safely inside the hard limits.
5. **Some LIVE-trading FTMO controls are NOT built yet** (the bot is in training/sim, not live):
   weekend-position auto-close, a minimum-trading-days *gate*, a hard per-trade 2% cap, and *enforced*
   funded-account consistency. The harness reports these as **WARNINGS / gaps** (honest), not bugs.
6. **JARVIS is the read-only council** (`src/jarvis/council.py` + `knowledge.py`). The harness tests
   that it can DIAGNOSE the seeded bug categories from its grounded knowledge (no live LLM required).
